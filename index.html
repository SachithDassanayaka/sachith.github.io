About Sachith.

I am Sachith Dassanayaka, Ph.D. candidate at the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Texas Tech University. I am a Graduate Part-Time Instructor, and I have taught Statistical Methods (Math 2300), Mathematical Statistics for Engineers and Scientists (Math 3342), Calculus I with Applications (Math 1451), Introductory Mathematical Analysis I (Math 1330), and Precalculus (Math 1550) courses for undergraduate students since Fall 2018.
My passion for finding answers for existing problems began when I was researching computer networks and security as an undergraduate student. These interests led to my desire to work in academia, and my research has run parallel to my teaching duties. My current interdisciplinary research is based on disinformation campaigns on social media platforms, and this research includes dimension reduction, machine learning, natural language processing, and neural networks.
